CONTRIBUTION 1: DETERMINISM CLASSES - NEW FRAMEWORK
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Problem Solved: How to formally measure "how deterministic" a model is?

Novel Definition:
  A model M belongs to determinism class D_ε if:
  sup_x E_θ[d(E_θ(x), E_θ'(x))] ≤ ε
  
  Translation: The maximum explanation difference across all random seeds ≤ ε

Classes:
  D_0   = Perfect determinism (impossible in practice)
  D_0.1 = High determinism (excellent)
  D_0.3 = Moderate determinism (acceptable)
  D_0.5+ = Unreliable (current state of models)

Why Novel?
  - NO prior work formally defines/measures determinism for explanations
  - Enables RANKING models by reproducibility
  - Makes determinism a measurable property like accuracy

Paper Value: 1 Figure (class visualization) + Theory = ~0.5 pages

CONTRIBUTION 2: CONVERGENCE THEOREM - THE MAIN CLAIM
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The Question: If I have deterministic explanations, do they converge to TRUE SAR?

Theorem (Informal):
  IF:
    - Model is accurate (learns SAR well)
    - Explanations are deterministic (D_0.1 class)
    - Explanations are faithful (masking important atoms changes predictions)
  THEN:
    - Explanations converge to true SAR (as you aggregate over molecules)

Mathematical Form:
  lim_{T→∞} E_x[d(E_T(x), E*(x))] → 0
  
  Where:
    E_T = explanation at time T (aggregating over runs)
    E* = true SAR (gradient of underlying SAR function)
    d = distance metric

Proof Strategy (Information Theory):
  1. Model learned Φ (SAR) → predictions capture truth
  2. Explanation deterministic → H(E) minimized (low noise)
  3. Explanation faithful → I(E; Φ) ≥ F × H(Φ)
     (Information between explanation and true SAR ≥ faithfulness × SAR complexity)
  4. By KL divergence → E must approximate ∇Φ

Why This Matters:
  - THEORETICAL GUARANTEE that determinism + faithfulness = discovery of true SAR
  - NO prior work provides this guarantee
  - Enables researchers to trust explanations when these conditions are met

Paper Value: 2-3 pages (definition, theorem, proof, intuition, corollaries)

CONTRIBUTION 4: INFORMATION-THEORETIC BOUNDS - FUNDAMENTAL LIMITS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The Insight: Complexity of explanations ≤ Complexity of true SAR

Formal Statement:
  H(Φ) ≤ min(H(E), F × H(Y))
  
  Where:
    H(Φ) = entropy of true SAR (how variable it is)
    H(E) = entropy of explanations (how much variation in importance scores)
    F = faithfulness
    H(Y) = entropy of labels (how imbalanced)

Implications:
  1. Simple explanations (low H(E)) → Only capture simple SAR
  2. Complex explanations → Must be unfaithful OR overfitted
  3. Determinism (low entropy) vs Model capacity (high complexity) = TRADE-OFF

Why Novel?
  - First formal information-theoretic characterization of explanation-SAR relationship
  - Explains why overly complex explanations are unreliable
  - Guides design: optimize for simplicity, not just accuracy

Paper Value: 1-2 pages (theorem, intuition, implications)
